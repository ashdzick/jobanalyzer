# Director of Product at Acme Corp
March 2026 | Resume: resume-product.pdf

## Fit Score: HIGH
Strong match on product leadership, AI delivery, and regulated-domain SaaS.
The title framing is the main risk — addressable in a cover letter.

## Personal Fit
No conflicts with stated preferences.

---

## Qualifications

| Qualification | Status | Evidence or Gap |
|---|---|---|
| 8+ years product leadership | `Strong` | Director-level portfolio ownership across multiple roles. Pattern is consistent. |
| B2B SaaS, regulated domain | `Strong` | Life insurance underwriting platform, healthcare supply chain. Both regulated. |
| AI-powered product delivery | `Partial` | One substantial AI project on record with enterprise guardrails. Agentic systems not demonstrated. |
| Directly manage Design and QA | `Partial` | Has managed designers across multiple roles. QA management not documented. |x
| Technical background | `Gap` | No engineering background on resume. Prototyping is design-tool based, not code-based. |
| UI/UX design background | `Strong` | 13+ years as a UX leader. Direct differentiator for this role. |

---

## Strategic Notes

Reframe your career narrative from Design Director to Product Leader who
uses design as a method, not an identity — the title gap is the biggest
risk and a cover letter that leads with "I have 13 years in UX" will get
screened out by anyone reading fast.

Address the AI gap by naming what you have done and connecting it to the
role's stated focus rather than leaving the reader to make that leap.
